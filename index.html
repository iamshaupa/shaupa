About Me

Results-driven Embedded & Network Engineering professional with 4.5+ years of experience delivering firmware, hardware design, and secure communication systems for IoT and ultra-low-power WiFi SoC platforms.

My work on Neutrino — an ultra-low-power WiFi SoC — involved compressing an entire security stack (OpenAuth through WPA3, MQTT over MBEDTLS, OTA) into just 512 KB SRAM. I thrive at the intersection of hardware constraints and network security.

Currently expanding into network infrastructure design, configuration, and security for critical communication environments, drawing directly from years of low-level protocol work and interrupt-driven architectures.
